# Strategic Assumptions

## H1: Portable chargers and AC chargers will continue to commoditize

Check:

- Price decline.
- RFQ trends.
- BOM transparency.
- Increasing suppliers.
- OEM pressure.
- Low-price bidding.

## H2: OEM/ODM remains the most realistic base business

Check:

- Automaker procurement demand.
- Portable charger attachment rate.
- Home charging package strategy.
- New RFQs.
- Customer concentration risk.

## H3: Zhida is a composite competitor, not just a low-price hardware supplier

Check:

- OEM customer expansion.
- Installation service network.
- Digital platform.
- Financial performance.
- Gross margin.
- Losses.
- Cash position.
- Overseas business.
- New products.
- Low-price bidding.

## H4: Bull should not be attacked directly in the C-end market

Check:

- Online ranking.
- Offline channel expansion.
- Installation service.
- Consumer brand strength.
- C-end pricing.

## H5: Small-power DC is an opportunity but not the short-term main battlefield

Check:

- 20/30/40kW demand.
- 4S store demand.
- Destination charging demand.
- Wattsaving activity.
- Customer orders.
- Installation and after-sales difficulty.

## H6: The biggest risks are customer concentration and normalized negative-margin orders

Check:

- Wuling pricing.
- XPeng pricing.
- Zhida entry into core customers.
- Keda Intelligent entry into core customers.
- New customer expansion progress.
- Margin deterioration.
