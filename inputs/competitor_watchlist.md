# Competitor Watchlist

## Zhida

Monitor:

- OEM customers.
- Wuling-related activity.
- Low-price bidding.
- Installation service.
- Digital platform.
- Financial performance.
- Overseas expansion.
- New products.

Strategic question: Is Zhida using structural cost advantage, service recovery, capital story, or strategic loss-making to win?

## Keda Intelligent

Monitor:

- OEM customers.
- Home charger volume.
- Quality certification.
- Low-price bidding.
- Entry into target customers.

Strategic question: Is Keda entering our target OEM customers or changing the price anchor?

## Bull

Monitor:

- C-end sales ranking.
- Offline channels.
- Installation service.
- Home charging products.
- Pricing.

Strategic question: Is Bull strengthening the channel and service moat enough that direct C-end attack remains unattractive?

## Star Charge

Monitor:

- Public charging operations.
- Destination charging.
- Small DC demand.
- Platform resources.
- Station resources.

Strategic question: Is small-power DC demand becoming tied to destination resources or platform operation capability?

## Wattsaving

Monitor:

- Small DC products.
- 20/30/40kW traction.
- Standards.
- New customers.
- Destination scenarios.

Strategic question: Is Wattsaving proving that small-power DC should move from order-driven pilot to priority product line?

## Tesla China

Monitor:

- Home charging service.
- Certified installers.
- Destination charging.
- Automaker ecosystem logic.

Strategic question: Does Tesla's ecosystem model change OEM home-charging expectations or installation service standards?
